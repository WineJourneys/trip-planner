1 About This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to Viva Travel Holdings Pty Ltd (ABN available on request), trading as Wine Journeys ("we", "us", "our"). It covers how we handle personal information in accordance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

By using our website, requesting a brochure, or making a booking with us, you agree to the collection and use of your personal information as described in this policy.

2 Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Identity information: name, date of birth, passport details, nationality
  • Contact information: email address, phone number, postal address
  • Booking information: tour selections, travel dates, number of travellers, payment records
  • Health and special needs information: dietary requirements, allergies, medical conditions, mobility restrictions (collected only where necessary for your safety and tour participation)
  • Emergency contact details: name, relationship, and contact number of a nominated emergency contact
  • Flight details: arrival and departure flight information and related travel arrangements
  • Financial information: payment records and transaction references (we do not store full credit card numbers)
  • Marketing preferences: email address and name provided when requesting a brochure or subscribing to updates

3 How We Collect Your Information

We collect personal information in the following ways:

  • Directly from you when you make a booking, complete a client portal form, or request a brochure via our website
  • Through email, phone, or written correspondence with our team
  • Via your use of our client portal (accessible at winejourneys.au/client/)
  • From third parties such as travel agents acting on your behalf (with your consent)

Where possible, we collect information directly from you. We only collect information that is necessary for the purposes of providing our services.

4 How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Process and manage your booking and payment schedule
  • Communicate with you about your tour, including updates, itinerary changes, and notifications
  • Provide your travel details to relevant suppliers (accommodation, transport, wineries, guides) to facilitate your tour
  • Manage dietary, medical, and mobility requirements on tour
  • Contact your emergency contact in the event of an incident
  • Send you marketing communications about upcoming tours and events (only where you have opted in or are an existing customer)
  • Improve our website, services, and client experience
  • Comply with legal obligations
We will not use your health or sensitive information for any purpose other than facilitating your safe participation in our tours.

5 Disclosure to Third Parties

We may share your personal information with the following categories of third parties, only to the extent necessary to deliver our services:

  • Tour suppliers: accommodation providers, wineries, transport companies, local guides, and dining venues involved in your tour
  • Travel agents: where a booking has been made through a third-party agent
  • Payment processors: to process deposits and tour payments securely
  • Government authorities: where required by law (e.g. border control, visa processing)
  • Professional advisers: lawyers, accountants, or insurers, where required

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

6 Overseas Disclosure

For international tours, your personal information may be disclosed to overseas-based tour suppliers (such as hotels, wineries, and transport providers in France, Italy, Portugal, South Africa, and other destinations). We take reasonable steps to ensure these recipients handle your information in a manner consistent with Australian privacy standards. By booking an international tour, you consent to this overseas disclosure.

7 Storage and Security

  • We store personal information securely using appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect against unauthorised access, misuse, loss, or disclosure.
  • Your client portal data is stored locally on secure devices managed by our team. Passport copies and sensitive documents are handled with restricted access.
  • We retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.
  • When personal information is no longer needed, we take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify it.

8 Marketing Communications

If you request a brochure or subscribe to our mailing list, we may send you information about our tours, special offers, and wine travel experiences. You can unsubscribe at any time by:

  • Clicking the unsubscribe link in any marketing email
  • Contacting us directly at tours@winejourneys.au

We will not send marketing communications to individuals who have opted out, and we do not share your contact details with third-party marketers.

9 Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience and to understand how visitors use our site. Cookies may collect non-identifiable information such as pages visited, time on site, and browser type.

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some areas of our website.

We may use third-party analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) to help us understand website usage. These services have their own privacy policies governing their use of data.

10 Access and Correction

Under the Australian Privacy Act, you have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of any inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated information

To make an access or correction request, please contact us in writing at tours@winejourneys.au. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before granting access. In some circumstances, we may decline a request where permitted by law, and will provide a written explanation.

11 Complaints

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information or breached the Australian Privacy Principles, you may lodge a complaint by contacting us at tours@winejourneys.au. We will acknowledge your complaint within 5 business days and aim to resolve it within 30 days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may refer your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

  • Website: www.oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992
